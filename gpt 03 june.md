## üî∂ 2. Alternative or Complementary Indicators for Soil Biodiversity

### 2.1. **Gene (Molecular) Diversity / ASV Richness**

- What: Number of **amplicon sequence variants (ASVs)** or **operational taxonomic units (OTUs)** in eDNA data.
    
- Reflects fine-scale diversity (especially for microbes, fungi).
    
- ‚úÖ Good for reproducibility and comparison across sites.
    
- ‚ùå May overestimate ‚Äúspecies‚Äù counts; hard to link directly to functional or ecosystem-level outcomes.
    

### 2.2. **Functional Diversity or Trait-Based Metrics**

- What: Number of different **functions or ecological roles** represented (e.g. decomposers, nitrifiers, predators).
    
- Based on taxon-function assignments from e.g. **FAPROTAX** (for bacteria), **FUNGuild** (fungi), or **trophic groupings** (macrofauna).
    
- ‚úÖ Closely linked to soil ecosystem functions (nutrient cycling, disease control).
    
- ‚úÖ Possibly less sensitive to taxonomy gaps.
    
- ‚ùå Requires robust functional annotation databases.
    

### 2.3. **Soil Function Surrogates**

- What: Direct measurement or proxy of **soil ecosystem functions**: e.g. decomposition rate, enzyme activity, respiration, nitrogen mineralization.
    
- ‚úÖ Ecologically meaningful; reflects consequences of biodiversity change.
    
- ‚ùå Not easy to generalize across regions or model spatially.
    
- ‚ùå Difficult to translate into LCA-compatible CFs unless linked to biodiversity proxies.
    

### 2.4. **Community Evenness or Entropy Metrics**

- What: e.g. **Shannon or Simpson diversity**, incorporating richness + abundance distribution.
    
- ‚úÖ More stable than just richness; less sensitive to rare taxa.
    
- ‚ùå May be harder to interpret ecologically in LCA unless linked to function.
    

### 2.5. **Multitrophic Diversity Indices**

- What: Aggregate richness or functional diversity across several groups (e.g. bacteria + fungi + nematodes).
    
- ‚úÖ Captures broad community-level effects of land use.
    
- ‚ùå Requires consistent sampling and marker coverage across groups.
    
- ‚úÖ Can be done from single eDNA sample with multiple primers.
